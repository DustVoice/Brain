# Methods

## Normal

The “intended” way of brewing an Aeropress _(whatever that's supposed to mean)_.

> [!tip]- It generally follows the following pattern
> 
> 1. Prepare the vessel
> 	1. Set up a scale
> 	2. Put your intended coffee vessel, suitable for pressing onto, on the scale
> 2. Prepare the Aeropress
> 	1. Put a paper filter into the Aeropress's Filter cap
> 	2. Screw on the cap
> 3. Prepare your grounds
> 	1. Weigh out your coffee beans
> 	2. Spritz them with a bit of water / stir with a lightly wet spoon _(to reduce static)_
> 	3. Grind
> 4. Put the Aeropress onto the vessel
> 5. Put your grounds into the Aeropress
> 6. Shake lightly to level the bed
> 7. Tare the scale
> 8. Start the timer
> 9. _Optional: Bloom_
> 	1. Poor the amount of water intended for blooming into the Aeropress
> 	2. _Optional: Stir lightly_
> 	3. Wait until the intended blooming timeframe has passed, then proceed
> 10. Pour in water until the desired water amount is reached
> 11. _Optional: Stir lightly_
> 12. Quickly put the plunger on, to create a vacuum to stop the dripping
> 13. Wait for a specified amount of time
> 14. _Optional: Swirl the container one time and wait 15-30s for the grounds to settle_
> 15. Gently push the plunger down, until it won't go further
> 16. Retract the plunger a tiny bit to prevent dripping
> 17. Turn the Aeropress around and set it aside
> 18. Enjoy

## Inverted

Brew without bypass. The _hip_ way to brew, but subjectively better. 

> [!tip]- It generally follows the following pattern
> 
> 1. **Boil** a kettle of water. During that time, **grind** your coffee, prepare the Aeropress, etc. for the *water to cool down slightly*
> 2. Dump in **all the brew water**, slowing down if you notice too much foam
> 3. **Stir for 1 min** continuously
> 4. **Wait for 1 min**
> 5. **Press**
> 6. Set up a scale
> 7. Put your intended coffee vessel, suitable for pressing onto, aside
> 8. Prepare the Aeropress
> 	1. Put the plunger into the Aeropress, until it _barely_ sits inside. **WARNING: If it's not securely inside, you might splurge hot water everywhere!**
> 	2. Put a paper filter into the Aeropress's Filter cap, but don't screw it on yet
> 9. Prepare your grounds
> 	1. Weigh out your coffee beans
> 	2. Spritz them with a bit of water / stir with a lightly wet spoon _(to reduce static)_
> 	3. Grind
> 10. Put the Aeropress upside down onto the scale so that the plunger is at the bottom
> 11. Put your grounds into the Aeropress
> 12. Shake lightly to level the bed
> 13. Tare the scale
> 14. Start the timer
> 15. _Optional: Bloom_
> 	1. Poor the amount of water intended for blooming into the Aeropress
> 	2. _Optional: Stir lightly_
> 	3. Wait until the intended blooming timeframe has passed, then proceed
> 16. Pour in water until the desired water amount is reached
> 17. Stir lightly
> 18. Take the Aeropress of the scale
> 19. Wait for the specified amount of time
> 20. Push the plunger further in, so the water surface is almost at the edge of the opening
> 21. Screw the filter cap on
> 22. In one swift motion, turn the Aeropress around and onto your vessel, while keeping a hand on the plunger, to make sure it doesn't go anywhere
> 23. _Optional: Swirl the container one time and wait 15-30s for the grounds to settle_
> 24. Gently push the plunger down, until it won't go further
> 25. Retract the plunger a tiny bit to prevent dripping
> 26. Turn the Aeropress around and set it aside
> 27. Enjoy
