Savage Worlds is a game system for [[TTRPG]]s.
Compared to its probably more popular and widely known system [[Dungeons & Dragons]], its more of a meta system, meant to be extended and specified by so called [[Attachments/Savage Worlds/The Player Guide (Shared)/1. Game Materials/Savage Setting]]s.
It's pretty easy to learn and adaptable to whatever genre and adventure you throw at it, be it the classical medieval/fantasy-esque campaign akin to [[Dungeons & Dragons\|DnD]], some space oddysey or sci-fi setting, some good ol' horror or even a superhero campaign.
It also has some very thoughtful properties and quality-of-life features, paired with a wider focus of situations and encounters than simple combat, like [[Attachments/Savage Worlds/The Player Guide (Shared)/4. Combat & Chase Quick Reference/Chases]], [[Networking]], [[Mass Battles]], [[Quick Encounters]] and more.

It is developed and published by [Pinnacle Entertainment Group](https://peginc.com), and many settings, adaptations and conversions from other system (most popular: [Savage Pathfinder](https://peginc.com/savage-settings/savage-pathfinder/)) exist not only from the publisher itself, but also countless by countless licensees, called [Aces](https://peginc.com/aces-licensees/).
It's a growing and blooming ecosystem.

There's also official content for major [[Virtual Tabletop]]s.
Although the core rules, settings books, companions and more are [available](https://peginc.com/?s=Foundry&post_type=product&search_limit_to_post_titles=0&add_woo_product_skus=0&fs=1) for [[Foundry VTT]], I currently use [[Fantasy Grounds]], which has almost every published module available.
SmiteWorks apparently doesn't charge upfront fees and instead shares revenue for developing their modules which results in a faster turnaround time for new releases at the cost of slightly higher purchase prices.
